Loggi is the newest unicorn

06/05/2019 at 16:00h

Startup that delivery documents, and even food has reached value of 1 billion dollars

The logistics startup Loggi has become the newest Brazilian unicorn, or startup valued at a billion dollars or more. The valuation came after a $ 150 million investment round made by investment funds SoftBank, GGV Capital, Fifth Wall and Velt Partners, as well as Microsoft.

The Brazilian delivery market is especially attractive: the country spends the equivalent of 12.7% of its Gross Domestic Product (GDP) in logistics, from storage to transportation. In the United States, this percentage is 7.8%. Currently, Loggi makes 100,000 daily deliveries and the goal is to deliver five million deliveries per day over the next five years. Including longer-term orders, Loggi makes 3 million deliveries a month in 33 cities, with 10,000 motorcycle drivers and vans drivers.

The resources will first serve to build a team of more than 1,000 developers. The investment in technology professionals began late last year with the purchase of the artificial intelligence startup applied to Internet advertising WorldSense. About 400 engineers were incorporated into the Loggi team at the time. The talent-focused acquisition movement, common in Silicon Valley (United States) in the years 2013 and 2014, is called acqui-hiring.

Another objective with the contribution of 150 million dollars is to expand the geographical performance of the company through points from where deliver the near deliveries to consumers. According to a previous interview with founder Fabien Mendez EXAME, 80% of the resources obtained in recent years were directed to identify and set up the delivery hubs. The company started this year with 12 such centers throughout the city of São Paulo, its headquarters.

History

Created in 2014 to send documents, Loggi has expanded and raised funds to meet new demands. It was the documents for e-commerce, taking advantage of the 12% growth of e-commerce in 2018, to 53.5 billion reais. Today, it serves the country's ten largest online stores, Como Dafiti and Mercado Livre. A year and a half ago, Loggi began delivering food. The idea is to enjoy different hours of action, intercalating deliveries of products from e-commerce with meals.

In all, Loggi has already raised $ 295 million in contributions. The latest contribution was made last October, also by SoftBank, worth 111 million dollars. The bet on delivery applications is nothing new for the investment fund of Japanese billionaire Masayoshi Son.

São Paulo integrates with other metropolises for delivery on the same or next day (model known as same or next day delivery). The region covered same or next day accounted for 35% of the national volume of orders at the beginning of the year. By 2020, the percentage should jump to 100%.
